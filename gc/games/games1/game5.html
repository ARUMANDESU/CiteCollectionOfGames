Half-Life 2

Genre: Action, Shooter
Platform: PC / Android / Mac / Linux / Xbox
Release date: 2004.11.16
Publisher: Vivendi Games
The developer: Valve Software

Half-Life 2 is a sequel to the cult action game Half-Life, which went on
sale 6 years after the original was released. Players again take control
of the scientist Gordon Freeman, who survived a cascading
resonance in the Black Mesa laboratory, who was put into stasis by a
mysterious man named G-Man. Gordon finds himself on Earth in the
near future, where all power belongs to an alien government that calls
itself the Alliance. The Alliance drove people into cities and forced
obedience, siphoning resources from the earth and suppressing the
human instinct to reproduce. Those who disagree are destroyed by
the Alliance detachments, so the fighters against the regime switched
to guerrilla warfare.

Gordon has a long way to go from City 17 station, the Alliance capital
on Earth, to his Black Mesa friends, who are located far outside the
city. Freeman's goal changes every time, but as a result, it is with his
hands that the rebels want to strike a decisive blow in the heart of the
alien invaders.

The gameplay is a first-person shooter in which players move through
various levels - here players travel around the city, and then they are
thrown into the depths of sewer tunnels. They are followed by a fast-
paced airboat race, a stroll through the extinct city of Ravenholm, an
assault on a fortified prison, and a battle with Alliance forces directly
in City 17. In addition to standard weapons, Gordon also has a special
gravity cannon, which allows him to manipulate physical objects,
throwing circular saws, barrels of fuel at opponents, and solving
puzzles with it.
